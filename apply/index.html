{% markdown %} ***Neuroscience Catalyst*** is seeking Letters of Intent from researchers investigating treatments for mood disorders and Alzheimer’s Disease. ### How to apply ### To submit a Letter of Intent for consideration, please download the Letter of Intent form, complete the form and receive sign off from your educational institution, as applicable. When you are ready click on and complete the Submission Form below to upload your completed Letter of Intent and short form Curriculum Vitae for the Primary Investigator. Submission deadline is __January 16, 2014 at 5 pm ET__. {% endmarkdown %}
Download Letter of Intent Form Submit Letter of Intent
{% markdown %} ### Rules ### {% endmarkdown %}
  • Letter of Intent must be submitted via the online application form before Tuesday, January 16th at 5:00 pm (ET) to be eligible.
  • Upon submission of your application the Joint Steering Committee will screen your application and Letter of Intent. Please note, follow up clarifying questions regarding the application or Letter of Intent may be asked of applicants after the application deadline.
{% markdown %} #### Review Criteria #### LOIs will be scored on the following bases: {% endmarkdown %}
     
  1. Disruptive nature of project (novelty, impact, achievability).
  2. Collaborative approach to innovation and ability to inject novel thinking & science. (Does the proposal consider the science beyond the applicant’s laboratory?)
  3. Attentiveness to healthcare with a direct impact to patient outcomes.
{% markdown %} #### Examples of Proposal Type and Funding Level/Duration of Funding #### {% endmarkdown %}
     
  1. Example 1 - a project funded for $78,900 for 9 months for in vitro (cell) target validation studies. Janssen could provide scientific guidance and expertise.
  2. Example 2 - a project funded for $234,500 for one year to develop a biomarker that could be translated into clinical use. Janssen could provide scientific guidance and expertise, and potentially patient samples to facilitate validation.
  3. Example 3 - a project funded for $345,600k for 24 months to optimize a cell assay for HTS screening, screen a small molecule library and validate hits in cell and potentially animal models. Janssen could provide in kind resources to enable the conduct of the HTS screen.
